The goal of the literary, film, and television horror genre is to frighten, shock, disgust, and sometimes even repulse its audience written by horror story writers. A horror story's primary goal, whether in a book, movie, or television program, is to shock the viewer with terrifying details. Story and characters are just as crucial to the horror genre as tone and atmosphere. A horror story's exploration of the unknown usually shocks and provokes.
